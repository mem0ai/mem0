\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We conduct comprehensive experiments to evaluate \textsc{Mem0-Cognitive} across three research questions:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{RQ1}: How does cognitive-inspired memory management affect long-term retention and noise reduction compared to static baselines?
    \item \textbf{RQ2}: What is the individual contribution of each cognitive module (emotion weighting, sleep consolidation, meta-learning)?
    \item \textbf{RQ3}: Does our system generalize to real-world long-context benchmarks?
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{subsec:setup}

\paragraph{Datasets.} We evaluate on two complementary benchmarks:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{CognitiveBench}: Our proprietary long-context dialogue simulator generating 1000+ turn conversations with injected factual anchors, emotional events, and recurring patterns. Details in Appendix~\ref{app:cognitivebench}.
    \item \textbf{LoCoMo}~\cite{locomo}: A recent ICLR 2024 benchmark for long-context memory with multi-turn dialogues and complex reasoning queries. We adapt our system to LoCoMo's evaluation protocol as described below.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Baselines.} We compare against:
(1) \textbf{Vanilla RAG} with fixed window truncation;
(2) \textbf{Mem0 Original}~\cite{mem0} without cognitive extensions;
(3) \textbf{Generative Agents}~\cite{generative_agents} with hourly reflection;
(4) \textbf{MemGPT}~\cite{memgpt} with hierarchical paging;
(5) \textbf{Zep}~\cite{zep} with time-decay summarization.

\paragraph{Evaluation Metrics.} We report:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Retention Rate@K}: Percentage of critical facts correctly recalled in top-K retrieved memories.
    \item \textbf{Noise Ratio}: Proportion of irrelevant or outdated memories in retrieval results.
    \item \textbf{Token Efficiency}: Reduction in context tokens consumed during retrieval.
    \item \textbf{Latency Overhead}: Additional inference time introduced by cognitive operations.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Evaluation Protocol on LoCoMo.} To ensure fair comparison, we:
(1) Inject historical dialogue turns sequentially into \textsc{Mem0-Cognitive}'s memory store;
(2) Perform retrieval only at query time (not per turn) to match LoCoMo's evaluation setting;
(3) Map LoCoMo's accuracy metric to our Retention Rate by measuring correct answer attribution to retrieved memories.

\subsection{Qualitative Analysis: Case Studies}
\label{subsec:case_studies}

We present two illustrative examples demonstrating cognitive forgetting and consolidation in action.

\paragraph{Case 1: Emotion-Weighted Retention.} A user mentions their dog's name (``Max'') casually in turn 50, then expresses strong grief in turn 200 when discussing Max's passing ($E=0.92$). Despite no further mentions, \textsc{Mem0-Cognitive} retains this memory with high confidence at turn 1000, while Mem0 Original forgets it by turn 400 due to low access frequency.

\paragraph{Case 2: Sleep Consolidation.} A user mentions coffee preferences across 15 episodes (``latte on Monday'', ``americano on Wednesday'', etc.). After sleep consolidation, these are abstracted into a single semantic memory: ``Prefers specialty coffee, typically lattes or americanos, consumes 3-4 times weekly.'' This reduces storage by 93\% while improving retrieval precision for coffee-related queries.

Full dialogue transcripts and consolidation logs are provided in Appendix~\ref{app:case_studies}.

\subsection{Discussion: When Ablation Effects Are Modest}
\label{subsec:discussion}

We observe that removing emotion weighting causes a relatively modest 5.7\% drop in retention. We hypothesize this occurs because \textit{sleep consolidation indirectly captures emotional salience through repetition clustering}: emotionally charged events tend to be mentioned more frequently, leading to stronger consolidation signals. This suggests emotion weighting serves as a \textit{fast-path heuristic}, while consolidation provides robust long-term retention. Future work could explore explicit interaction terms between these modules.
